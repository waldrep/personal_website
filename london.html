From September 9th until December 12th 2019, I lived in London, thanks to the Richard Rogers Fellowship from the Harvard Graduate School of Design. I was there to walk, photograph, and understand the outer edges of the city, as I had done in Mexico City in 2014-15. If that latter city, as I argue in my project La Ciudad Actual, is in many ways the future of growing and suburbanizing cities throughout the world, then London, I would argue, is the original seat of planned suburbia.

From Ebenzer Howard’s ideal of the Garden City, designed and to some extent built at the turn of the last century, beginning at Letchworth, all the way through the publicly-funded New Town movement after the end of the second World War, expansion and de-densification was how London grew. The variegated aesthetics and politics of this diffusion define the current face of the city and its region, and have reverberated internationally.
