Michael Waldrep is an artist & researcher based in Berlin and Los Angeles.

Through video, cartography, writing, and photography, his practice is concerned with architecture, urban design, and the ways in which societies create and occupy space.

MW studied in Film and City Planning at UC Berkeley and MIT, respectively, and has lectured in Europe and North America on the intersection of the two fields. He has been awarded the Fulbright–National Geographic fellowship for research in Mexico City and the Harvard GSD–Richards Rogers fellowship for work in London. From 2017-2022, he was a member of Studio Olafur Eliasson in Berlin.

Since 2020, he's been teaching narrative & video at the architecture department of the Universität Kassel. In 2024, he’s pursuing freelance work and elaborating a project on the built traces of the offshore economy in Luxembourg, South Dakota, and Delaware.
